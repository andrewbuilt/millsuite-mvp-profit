What this page is — and what's manual for V1

Approval items are derived from estimate line callouts. Each one is a single decision: what material + what finish. Construction details (door style, edge profile, drawer joinery, dimensions, hardware quantities) live on the production drawing, not here. If a drawing changes, the drawings track moves — this page doesn't pretend to mirror it.

A subproject can't move to scheduling until every item AND its drawings are marked approved. A ball-in-court timer surfaces who's holding things up. When a client picks a different material, a change order is drafted as an estimate-line diff — original line on the left, proposed line on the right, edit the spec to reprice. Sometimes there's no price change at all (just check the box).

Everything else stays manual for V1. No portal signing. No email automation. No auto-pushing to QuickBooks — the down payment on the original invoice + repeated material flip-flops is too much risk. Client approval is a status field you mark by hand after talking to them. QuickBooks reconciliation is a manual step in QB. We pick a pattern (edit invoice vs. separate CO invoice) once a few real COs have run through. Automation comes later, not first.
